Salon Manager — Functional Design

What the system does, for whom, and by what rules. Feature-by-feature functional specification, business rules, states, and end-to-end workflows.

Covers two Naturals salons and one FirstCry store operating under a single company, sharing one billing system and the "Gems" loyalty programme.

1. Purpose & Scope

Salon Manager runs the day-to-day operations of multiple outlets from one system: taking payments, tracking inventory, rewarding customers, scheduling and paying staff, and controlling who can see what.

In scope: point-of-sale billing with GST, service & retail catalogues, price books, inventory with purchase orders and stock audits, vendors, a four-tier loyalty programme, appointments, expenses, staff records, shifts & weekly rosters, attendance with manager approval, salary advances, incentive profiles, payroll, and role-based access across an organisation tree.

Out of scope (today): automated reorder suggestions, tax filing/e-invoicing, SMS/WhatsApp notifications, online customer bookings, and payment-gateway integration. Payments are recorded, not processed.

4. Billing & Invoicing

4.1 What it does

A cashier builds a bill for a customer visit: looks the customer up by phone, adds line items (services and/or products), assigns the staff member to each line, applies a discount and/or tip, optionally redeems the customer's Gems, chooses a payment mode, and saves — which generates a printable invoice and updates stock and loyalty.

4.2 Functional rules

  • Customer lookup by phone. If found, the name, tier badge and Gem balance appear. If not found, the cashier can register the customer inline.
  • Line items. Each line is a service or a product, with quantity, unit price, GST%, and an assigned staff member. Services may also record a professional product consumed (deducted from stock at zero sale value).
  • Pricing. A selected price book overrides default service prices; otherwise the service's default price is used.
  • Discount can be a flat amount or a percentage. Tip is added on top.
  • Gem redemption. The cashier enters Gems to redeem; the system converts them to a rupee discount (see §5) and caps redemption at the available balance.
  • Grand total = services subtotal + services GST + retail subtotal + retail GST − (discount + redemption) + tip.
  • Payment modes: Cash, Card, UPI, or Split (amounts across all three). Split must balance to the grand total.
  • On save: the invoice is generated, stock is deducted for products, and Gems are redeemed/earned and the tier recalculated.
Validation The Save button is blocked until there is a customer, at least one complete line with a staff member and quantity > 0, and (for Split) a balanced payment. Validation is enforced in the browser.

5. Loyalty Programme — "Gems"

5.1 Concept

Customers earn Gems on eligible spend and climb through four tiers. The programme is shared across all outlets — Gems earned at a Naturals salon can be redeemed at FirstCry and vice-versa. It blends two ideas: a status that reflects loyalty over the last 12 months, and a separate spendable balance.

5.2 Two balances

Spendable balance
Gems available to redeem for money off. Goes up when you earn, down when you redeem.
Status points
Your tier score — the total Gems earned in the rolling last 12 months. Redeeming never reduces your status. This is what keeps a loyal customer in a high tier even if they spend their Gems.

5.3 Earning

Gems earned = floor(eligible spend ÷ 100) × base rate × tier multiplier × happy-hour multiplier. Only spend in service/product groups flagged as points-eligible counts. Higher tiers earn faster (multiplier); happy hour multiplies further.

5.4 Tiers

  • Four configurable tiers, each with a name, a status-point threshold, an earn multiplier, and a badge colour.
  • You qualify for the highest tier whose threshold your status points reach.
  • Downgrade protection: if your status falls, you drop at most one tier per recalculation — never straight to the bottom.

5.5 Redemption

  • Configurable conversion: every N Gems (redemption rate) = ₹V off (redemption value). A minimum-redemption threshold can be configured.
  • Redemption is applied as a discount on the bill and deducted from the spendable balance. It never touches status points.

5.6 Expiry & Happy Hour

  • Expiry: each Gem earned expires after a configurable number of months (default 12) from its earn date. Expired Gems no longer count toward status.
  • Happy Hour: a bonus-multiplier window. It can be switched on manually at any time, and/or run on a recurring weekly schedule (chosen days, time window, optional effective-from/until dates).

6. Catalogues & Pricing

6.1 Services & Service Groups

Services belong to a group. The group carries the tax and commercial settings: GST%, SAC code, whether it counts toward staff revenue targets, a direct-incentive percentage, loyalty eligibility, and display order. A service itself carries only its default price and duration.

6.2 Products & Product Groups

Products are Retail (sold to customers) or Professional (consumed during services). The group carries GST%, HSN code, a per-unit staff incentive, and loyalty eligibility. Each product tracks cost, retail price, current stock, and a base/reorder level.

6.3 Price Books

A price book is a named set of service prices, letting different outlets or customer segments use different pricing. For any service, the price book uses its override if set, otherwise the service's default price.

7. Inventory & Purchasing

  • Purchase Orders capture what was ordered from a vendor. A PO starts as draft, becomes partial as goods arrive, and received once every line is fully received.
  • Receive Stock records goods coming in — against a PO or as a direct receipt — increasing stock and logging a receipt movement.
  • Stock Register is the complete movement history: receipts (+), sales/usage from bills (−), and audit adjustments (±).
  • Stock Audit compares physical counts to system quantities, records the variance, and corrects the stock to the counted figure.
Note Stock is allowed to go negative so the register always reconciles with reality; managers use the audit to correct drift. Automated reorder suggestions are not currently provided.

8. Appointments

Appointments are booked per day for a customer, staff member and service, with a start time and duration. They can be viewed as a list or a timeline, navigated day by day, and carry a status through their lifecycle (booked → completed/cancelled). An appointment can be linked to the bill it generated.

9. Expenses

Outlet expenses are recorded with a date, category, vendor, amount, payment mode and reference number. They can be filtered by period, category and payment mode, summarised, and voided if entered in error.

10. Staff & HR

10.1 Staff records

Each staff member has personal details, role, salary, allowance, specialisation, an incentive profile, a target period (weekly or monthly), and a portal PIN. If no PIN is set, the default is the last four digits of their phone.

10.2 Shifts & Weekly Schedule

Shifts define working hours. The weekly schedule assigns each staff member a shift and their off-days for a given week (Monday-anchored). Only current and future weeks are meant to be edited.

10.3 Attendance

  • Each day is marked present, absent or half-day, with clock-in/out times.
  • Overtime = hours worked beyond 9 in a day.
  • Staff can self-log attendance from their portal; it enters a pending state until a manager approves or rejects it. Managers can also enter attendance directly.

10.4 Advances

Salary advances are tracked with a running balance. Staff request an advance (pending → approved → disbursed), or a manager records one directly as disbursed. Disbursed advances are deducted at payroll.

10.5 Comp Plans

A comp plan defines how a staff member earns commission: an OT hourly rate, two revenue targets (T1/T2), and three commission brackets (X% up to T1, Y% between T1 and T2, Z% above T2). Targets can be fixed amounts or a percentage of salary, and can be based on the individual's revenue or the whole org's.

11. Payroll

For a staff member and month, payroll computes:

  • Base & leave: excess leaves beyond the eligible free offs are deducted pro-rata from salary. Weekend absences (Fri/Sat/Sun) count double; weekend half-days count as a full day.
  • Overtime pay = OT hours × the profile's OT rate.
  • Incentives: service, product and target/direct incentives — summed weekly or computed monthly from bills, depending on the staff member's target period.
  • Net pay = adjusted base + allowances + OT pay + total incentive − advances deducted.

Payroll is calculated as a draft, reviewed, then saved; its status can be advanced (e.g. to paid).

12. Organisation & Access Control

  • Organisations form a parent → child tree (company → outlets). An org cannot be deleted while it has children.
  • Users belong to an org and a role. A parent-org user sees their whole branch; a leaf-org user sees only their outlet.
  • Roles group permissions. Permissions are per-role, per-menu-item access flags.
  • Emails must be unique across users.

13. Self-Service Portals

13.1 Customer portal ("My Gems")

A customer enters their phone number (no password) and sees their tier, Gem balance, progress toward the next tier, and full visit history with the Gems earned/redeemed on each visit. Designed to be delightful and shareable.

13.2 Staff portal

Staff log in with phone + PIN and can: confirm the bill items credited to them (so incentives are accurate), log their attendance, request advances, and change their PIN. It uses a session independent of the admin app.
